Our Story

The Al-Salam Palace Museum, located in Kuwait's Bayan Palace complex, is a cultural landmark that preserves the nation's royal heritage and Islamic artistic legacy. Originally built as a royal guest palace in 1987 to host distinguished visitors like Queen Elizabeth II, the museum was repurposed in 2016 to showcase Kuwait's rich history through exhibitions of Islamic art, royal artifacts, and historical documents. Established under the patronage of His Highness the Amir, the museum serves as both an educational institution and a symbol of Kuwait's commitment to safeguarding its cultural identity while fostering appreciation for Islamic civilization's artistic achievements. Through its collections and programs, Al-Salam Palace Museum bridges Kuwait's past with its present, offering visitors immersive insights into the region's traditions and royal history.
